Garden Setup
Plant Guide

Garden type

In-ground: Direct soil, most space. Best for veggies like tomatoes and peppers.

Raised beds: Better drainage and soil control. Easier on your back.

Containers: Pots and window boxes. Ideal for herbs and small veggies; portable.

Materials checklist

  • Pots or raised bed frame
  • Potting mix or garden soil
  • Compost
  • Seeds or seedlings
  • Mulch (straw, wood chips)
  • Watering can or hose
  • Basic hand trowel

Soil & drainage

Use well-drained potting mix for containers. Add compost for nutrients. Avoid heavy clay without amendment. Ensure pots have drainage holes.

Layout grouping

High-water group: Basil, mint, parsley, lettuce, spinach—plant together for easier watering.

Low-water group: Rosemary, thyme, oregano—keep away from heavy water to avoid root rot.

Mint containment warning

Grow mint in a pot only. Mint spreads aggressively via rhizomes and will take over beds. Use a separate container or sink a pot into the ground.

Maintenance guide

Water in the morning. Pinch herbs to encourage branching. Remove dead leaves. Harvest regularly. Mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
